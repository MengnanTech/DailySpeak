# Stage 1 Content Standard

## Purpose
Stage 1 teaches learners to build clear, natural 1-2 minute answers for everyday IELTS-style speaking prompts. The baseline target is stable Band 6.5-7.5 output, with a controlled layer of Band 8.0 upgrades that still sound teachable and realistic.

## Core Rules
- Prioritize natural spoken English over impressive-looking vocabulary.
- Keep each lesson reusable: learners should be able to swap their own details into the framework.
- Avoid over-drama, overclaiming, and one-size-fits-all "high score" lines.
- Every upgrade expression must improve precision, tone, or rhythm, not just complexity.

## Required Blocks
Every Stage 1 lesson must include:
- `topic`
- `strategy`
- `framework`
- `vocabulary`
- `phrases`
- `samples`
- `practice`

## Strategy Rules
- Provide exactly 4 angles so the learner can build content before speaking.
- Provide a 3-step speaking sequence that fits a 1-2 minute answer.
- Provide a 3-part content ratio with labels that match the topic.
- Include high-score tips, content mistakes, and language mistakes.
- Language mistakes must reflect real learner errors, not invented textbook examples.

## Sample Rules
- Provide 3 sample answers:
  - Band 6.5-7.0: stable and simple
  - Band 7.0-7.5: clearer detail and better cohesion
  - Band 8.0+: more precise rhythm and reflection
- Suggested answer length:
  - Band 6: 110-140 words
  - Band 7: 140-175 words
  - Band 8: 170-210 words
- Upgrades should be explainable in plain language.

## Banned Patterns
- Fake-native claims like "this is how natives always say it"
- Empty high-level phrases without context
- Extreme emotional wording unless the topic clearly needs it
- Long memorized openings that do not answer the prompt
- Chinese-to-English direct translation patterns that sound stiff or literal

## Review Checklist
- Schema is complete.
- Prompt, strategy, vocabulary, phrases, and samples match the same scenario.
- All 3 samples feel like spoken English, not essays.
- No preview/demo/temporary wording remains.
- The lesson still reads naturally if the learner swaps in personal details.
